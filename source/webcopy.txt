Rebuild this around your uploaded deck, not around the current site. The strongest story in the PDF is: **Bangladesh opportunity → conversion gap → Moddin’s role → services → process → partner types**. That should become the website backbone. 

From competitor benchmarking, the useful pattern is clear: **BIDA** organizes around opportunities, services, resources, and direct inquiry flows like “Ask a Question,” “Book an Appointment,” and OSS; **Invest India** combines sector pages, services, success stories, and a resource repository; **IDA Ireland** leads with a sharp partner-oriented headline plus sector proof; **Enterprise Singapore** makes market entry support feel concrete through clear service buckets. For Moddin, the right move is to borrow that **clarity and structure**, but keep a more private-sector, execution-led tone than a public authority website. ([BIDA][1])

## Recommended site structure

**Top nav**
Home | Why Bangladesh | Services | Who We Work With | Insights | Contact
Primary CTA: **Book an Intro Call**

This is the BIDA-style clarity you want, but simplified for Moddin.

* Priority sectors / opportunity clusters

* Who it is for
* What outcome it supports
* Related CTA

### Who We Work With

Split by:

* Global companies
* Investors & funds
* Institutions
* Bangladeshi companies

Each audience page should answer:

* Why they come to Moddin
* What challenges they face
* What Moddin helps them achieve

### Insights

Use as a serious trust-building page:

* Bangladesh briefs
* Sector notes
* Trade mission updates
* Market entry explainers

### Contact

Take the useful part of BIDA’s pattern here: **ask a question + book a meeting**. That structure works because it gives low-friction and high-intent contact options. ([BIDA][1])

## Copy rules for the whole site

**1. Keep headlines outcome-led**
Say “Bangladesh, Made Easier to Enter” instead of generic lines like “Connecting Bangladesh to Global Businesses.”

**2. Keep each section to one idea**
Do not stack country promotion, services, and proof into one paragraph.

**3. Use fewer adjectives, more mechanics**
Prefer “market entry, partner access, execution support” over “trusted, innovative, world-class.”

**4. Homepage should sell clarity, not explain everything**
The deck can stay expansive. The website should be decisive.

**5. Borrow BIDA’s structure, not BIDA’s voice**
Moddin should sound like an execution partner, not a government portal. That is the right differentiation based on the deck and the benchmark set.  ([BIDA][1])
