ii. The Gap

High Potential.
Still Hard to Unlock.

Interest in Bangladesh is strong. Conversion into real investment and partnerships is where friction begins — and where Moddin comes in.

i. What Bangladesh already has

Scale, export strength, industrial depth, and cost advantage.

A proven manufacturing base, a young and ambitious workforce, and two decades of consistent export-led growth.

ii. What investors still face

Fragmented access, regulatory complexity, and difficulty finding the right local pathways.

Navigating ministries, chambers, and capital partners without trusted intermediaries slows every decision.

iii. What this leads to

Delayed decisions, missed opportunities, and weaker market entry outcomes.

Strong interest stalls short of execution — not because the thesis is wrong, but because the path isn't clear.
